# Travertine Research

Research and design documentation for a Mammoth Hot Springs-style travertine terrace simulation.

## Overview

This repository contains research into the geology, chemistry, and physics of travertine formation at Mammoth Hot Springs (Yellowstone National Park), with the goal of building a realistic simulation/game.

## Key Insight

Mammoth Hot Springs is not just a chemical precipitation system. It is a **tectonically-driven geothermal-chemical factory** where the heat source, chemical source, transport mechanism, and surface expression are all consequences of specific fracture geometry created by caldera collapse ~631,000 years ago.

The Norris-Mammoth fault corridor connects the Norris Geyser Basin (heat source) to limestone bedrock (chemical source) to the surface terraces (depositional expression). Without this fault, you have a puddle. With it, you have a landscape.

## Simulation Concept

A game where players place geothermal wells on a preset tectonic map, controlling flow rate and watching travertine terraces emerge through:
- CO₂ degassing-driven supersaturation
- Surface-tension-limited terrace height (~4-5 mm steps)
- Hydrodynamic regime changes (pools vs thin-sheet rims)
- Seismic events that shift vents and redirect flow
- Microbial colonization creating color banding
