# References

## Academic Papers

- Hammer et al. (2007). "The dynamics of travertine dams." *Earth Surface Processes and Landforms*. 
  - Uses depth-averaged hydrodynamic code coupled with empirical precipitation rate
  - Identifies surface tension as the controlling factor for terrace height (~4-5 mm)
  - Proposes geometric scaling law: IDD = h tan(α) + k

- Hammer et al. (2010). "Travertine terracing: patterns and mechanisms." In *Tufa and Speleothem Science*.
  - Reviews terrace formation mechanisms
  - Discusses biological influence on morphology

- Pentecost, A. (2005). *Travertine*. Springer.
  - Comprehensive monograph on travertine geology

- Liu et al. (2023). "Effective mechanisms in the formation of pool-rimstone dams in continental carbonate systems: The case study of Huanglong, China." *Sedimentology*.
  - Computational fluid dynamics approach to tufa hydrodynamics
  - Biological factors in fabric and morphology

1. Mammoth is not just chemical precipitation — it's a tectonically-driven system
2. The Norris-Mammoth fault corridor is the critical plumbing
3. Terrace height (~4-5 mm) is controlled by surface tension, not flow rate
4. CO₂ degassing is the primary precipitation driver, not evaporation
5. Aragonite vs calcite depends on temperature
6. Microbial mats create the color banding
7. Seismic events actively reshape the terraces
