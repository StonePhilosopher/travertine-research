# Geology of Mammoth Hot Springs

## Tectonic Setting

Mammoth Hot Springs sits at the intersection of three major tectonic features:

### 1. Yellowstone Caldera Rim (~631,000 years old)
- The caldera collapse created a ring fracture system that channels heat upward
- Without this caldera, there would be no plumbing
- The fracture network connects deep magma to the surface

### 2. Hebgen Lake Fault System
- East-west fault extending from Montana into the park
- 1959 M7.4 earthquake reactivated thermal features
- Continues to influence geyser behavior and vent positions

### 3. Norris-Mammoth Corridor
- **North-south fault zone** that funnels heat from the caldera toward Mammoth
- Connects Norris Geyser Basin to Mammoth area
- Acts as a geothermal pipeline

## The Norris-Mammoth Fault Corridor

This is the critical feature for travertine formation:

- **Channels magmatic heat** from the Yellowstone caldera's underlying magma chamber (~459°F/237°C at 1,087 feet depth at Norris)
- **Routes superheated water through Paleozoic limestone bedrock**, dissolving calcium carbonate along the way
- **Creates hydraulic head** that drives continuous upward flow
- **Controls chemistry** — water cools from ~200°F+ at Norris to ~170°F at Mammoth, affecting mineralogy

## Heat Source

- Norris Geyser Basin: superheated water reaching ~200°F+
- Research well at Norris recorded 459°F (237°C) at 1,087 feet depth
- Shallow circulation along the Norris-Mammoth corridor allows cooling before surfacing

## Chemical Source

- Paleozoic limestone formations along the fault corridor
- Water dissolves CaCO₃ as it travels through the limestone
- Pickup rate depends on temperature, pCO₂, and residence time

## Water Chemistry

- **Temperature at surface:** ~170°F (80°C)
- **pH:** Alkaline (not acidic like Norris's acid-sulfate features)
- **Dominant ions:** Ca²⁺, HCO₃⁻
- **Saturation state:** Supersaturated with respect to CaCO₃ at surface conditions

## Seismic Controls

Minor earthquakes:
- Shift spring vents (kill old terraces, create new ones)
- Redirect flow paths
- Change flow rates
- Open or seal fracture permeability

**Example:** The Minerva Terrace went dry because earthquake activity shifted the spring vent.

## Terraces as Tectonic Products

The Mammoth terraces are not just chemical deposits. They are the **surface expression of a deep tectonic process**:

```
Magma Chamber → Caldera Fractures → Norris-Mammoth Fault → Limestone Dissolution → Surface Discharge → CO₂ Degassing → CaCO₃ Precipitation
```

Without the fault corridor connecting these elements, you have hot water OR limestone OR a surface spring. You do not have Mammoth Hot Springs.
